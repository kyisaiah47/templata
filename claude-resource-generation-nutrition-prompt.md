## Quality Benchmarks for Nutrition Content

### Authority Indicators
- Reference current dietary guidelines and nutrition research
- Include evidence-based recommendations from reputable nutrition organizations
- Demonstrate knowledge of various dietary approaches and their applications
- Show understanding of individual differences and personalization needs
- Reference registered dietitian standards and professional nutrition practices

### Practical Value Metrics
- Provide specific meal planning templates and recipe frameworks
- Include actionable shopping lists and preparation strategies
- Offer decision frameworks for dietary choices and food selection
- Give realistic timelines for meal prep and cooking processes
- Include cost analysis and budget-friendly nutrition strategies

### Professional Credibility Tests
- Would registered dietitians and nutrition professionals recommend this resource?
- Does this include insights known primarily to nutrition and culinary professionals?
- Are the nutrition recommendations evidence-based and safe?
- Does this solve real challenges people face in meal planning and healthy eating?
- Would this prevent common nutrition mistakes and support sustainable healthy habits?

## Content Differentiation Requirements

### Never Create Generic Content About
- Basic nutrition advice available in countless diet and wellness blogs
- One-size-fits-all meal plans ignoring individual preferences and needs
- Fad diet promotion or unsustainable eating approaches
- Nutrition misinformation or claims not supported by current science

### Always Create Definitive Resources For
- Evidence-based nutrition strategies with current dietary research
- Professional-level meal planning that addresses real-life constraints
- Comprehensive cooking guidance that builds confidence and skills
- Individualized approaches that respect dietary preferences and medical needs
- Sustainable strategies that promote long-term healthy eating habits

## Success Validation for Nutrition Articles

**Each article must pass these tests:**
1. **Professional Standards**: Would registered dietitians and nutrition experts endorse this?
2. **Evidence-Based**: Are recommendations supported by current nutrition science?
3. **Practical Sustainability**: Can individuals realistically implement and maintain these strategies?
4. **Individual Adaptability**: Does this account for different dietary needs and preferences?
5. **Holistic Health**: Does this promote overall wellness rather than restrictive eating?

**The Ultimate Test**: If someone could only read one resource about this nutrition topic, would this guide them to develop sustainable, healthy eating habits that support their overall wellbeing while fitting their lifestyle and preferences?
